Research fellow

Currently, I'm a research fellow in air quality at the University of Leeds, UK. My focus is on understanding how policies can improve air quality and reduce the impact on human health in India, China, Indonesia, and South America.

This interactive map shows how clean household energy can improve public health in India here.

PhD

I completed a PhD in Ambient air quality and human health in India from the University of Leeds, UK, in 2018. My supervisors were Dominick Spracklen, Stephen Arnold, Christoph Knote, and Alan Williams. I was part of the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Bioenergy.

  • I quantified the contribution of different sources to the disease burden from ambient air pollution exposure in India and the effects of future air pollution control pathways.
  • I wrote 3 first-author papers and presented at 15 conferences.
  • My work was the focus of 4 research spotlight articles (2 for EOS: Earth and Space Science News here and here, 1 for the National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences of the United States Government here, and 1 for the University of Leeds homepage here).
  • I was a postgraduate researcher publication prize winner from the School of Earth and Environment, University of Leeds, UK, in 2018.
  • I taught air pollution and human health to undergraduates. Take a look here at this interactive presentation to find out more.
